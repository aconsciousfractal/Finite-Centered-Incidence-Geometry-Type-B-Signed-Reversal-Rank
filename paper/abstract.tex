We study a noncentral signed-reversal incidence layer in the signed permutation group.  For the diagonal layer
$\BtildeI_n(k,k)$, the aggregate operators on the two ambient standard channels have exact ranks
$\lceil n/2\rceil$ and $\lceil n/2\rceil-1$ whenever the layer is nonempty.  The rank numbers are classical
matching-scheme multiplicities; the object-specific content is the collapse of the signed-reversal layer onto the
reversal idempotent and the positivity of the resulting scalars.  We also record a positive-only separator add-on:
for $X_n=\BtildeI_n(1,1)$ and $n\ge4$, the layer is neither a signed-poset linear-extension set nor an exact
left-translate tiler of $B_n$.
