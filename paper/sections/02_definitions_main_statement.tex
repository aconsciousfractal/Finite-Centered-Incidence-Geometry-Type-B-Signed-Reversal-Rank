\section{Definitions and Main Statement}\label{sec:defs}

Let $\Bn$ be the signed permutation group, written as pairs $(\pi,\varepsilon)$ with $\pi\in S_n$ and
$\varepsilon_i\in\{\pm1\}$.  We use zero-indexed coordinates and set
\[
  \rho(i)=n-1-i.
\]
Define
\[
\BtildeI_n(k,\ell)=\{(\pi,\varepsilon)\in \Bn:
  \#\{i:\pi(i)=i,\varepsilon_i=+1\}=k,
  \#\{i:\pi(i)=\rho(i),\varepsilon_i=+1\}=\ell\}.
\]
The theorem concerns only the diagonal layer $\BtildeI_n(k,k)$ and only the two ambient standard-channel aggregate
operators $M_{\mathrm{ref}}$ and $M_{\mathrm{pair\text{-}std}}$.

\begin{theorem}[Main theorem]\label{thm:main}
For every $n\ge2$ and every $k\ge1$ such that $\BtildeI_n(k,k)$ is nonempty,
\[
  \rank M_{\mathrm{ref}}(\BtildeI_n(k,k))=\left\lceil\frac n2\right\rceil,
  \qquad
  \rank M_{\mathrm{pair\text{-}std}}(\BtildeI_n(k,k))=\left\lceil\frac n2\right\rceil-1.
\]
\end{theorem}

The same two rank equalities hold on the type-D parity sublayer $\DtildeI_n(k,k)=\BtildeI_n(k,k)\cap D_n$; this is
Theorem~\ref{thm:typed} in Section~\ref{sec:typed}.

\begin{remark}[Boundary]
No full $B_n$ fingerprint theorem, enumeration novelty claim, or general Weyl theorem is asserted.
The positive-fixed marginal is treated as classical signed-permutation enumeration; in particular the
positive-fixed zero row is the type-B derangement sequence \cite{OEISA000354,ChenZhang2007}.  The rank number and the
reversal projector are matching-scheme bookkeeping \cite{Delsarte1973,BannaiIto1984,GodsilRoyle2001}.  The contribution here is the
collapse and scalar positivity for the specific noncentral layer $\BtildeI_n(k,k)$ and its type-D parity sublayer, plus
the separate separator theorem below for $\BtildeI_n(1,1)$.
\end{remark}
