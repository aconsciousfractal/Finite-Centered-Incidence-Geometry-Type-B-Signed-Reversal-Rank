# Claim Boundary

## Public-Safe Claims

1. For every nonempty diagonal row `B~I_n(k,k)`, `n>=2`, `k>=1`, the two ambient standard-channel ranks are `ceil(n/2)` and `ceil(n/2)-1`.
2. The type-D parity sublayer `D~I_n(k,k) = B~I_n(k,k) ∩ D_n` (`D_n = ker(chi)`) has the same two standard-channel ranks `ceil(n/2)` and `ceil(n/2)-1`, on the same channels and the same diagonal cells.
3. For `X_n=B~I_n(1,1)`, `n>=4`, the positive-only layer is not a Reiner-style signed-poset linear-extension set and is not an exact left-translate tiler of `B_n`; `n=3` is an exact tiling exception.
4. The finite `n=8` appendix gives exact zero mechanisms and selected finite defect-family audits.

## Not Claimed

- No full native `B_n` fingerprint theorem.
- No full fingerprint classification.
- No all-irrep saturation theorem.
- No general Weyl or affine extension theorem beyond the type-D parity sublayer stated above.
- No enumeration novelty claim.

The type-D claim is confined to the same two ambient standard channels and the same diagonal cells as the main theorem; no other reflection subgroup or channel is treated. The finite full-fingerprint appendix is evidence and boundary analysis, not an expansion of the main theorem.
